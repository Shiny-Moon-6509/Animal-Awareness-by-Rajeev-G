Extinct Animals

Extinction is the cessation of a species or group of species, usually a species. The moment of extinction is generally considered the death of the last individual of the species, but the ability to reproduce and recover before that may be lost. point.

List of a few endangered species

1. The Dodo
Dodo is an extinct flightless bird endemic to Mauritius Island, east of Madagascar in the Indian Ocean. The closest genetic relative of the Dodo was the also extinct Rodrigues solitaire.

2. Tasmanian Tiger
Thylacine is an extinct carnivorous marsupial native to mainland Australia and Tasmania and New Guinea. The last known living animal was captured in Tasmania in 1930. It is commonly known as the Tasmanian tiger or the Tasmanian thylacine.

3. West African Black Rhino
The West African Black Rhinoceros is an extinct subspecies of the Black Rhinoceros. It was declared extinct by IUCN in 2011. The Western Black Rhinoceros was thought to be genetically different from other rhino variants.

4. Aurochs
The Aurochs was once a type of bison that roamed vast areas of Europe, Asia, and North Africa. This cow-like species was one of the most common grazing animals on Earth thousands of years ago. However, due to population pressures from humans and livestock, their numbers have been gradually reduced to a small extent. The last known Aurochs died in central Poland in 1627. But the bison has captured the human imagination so much that numerous attempts have been made to resurrect the extinct species from the dead.
